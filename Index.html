Our Product

VyoM

An Ultra Low-Cost High-Precision GNSS Rover and Base. VyoM is a cutting-edge, cost-effective GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System) solution designed for high-precision positioning applications.
It offers both rover and base station functionality, providing centimeter-level accuracy through RTK (Real-Time Kinematic) corrections.
VyoM can receive RTK corrections via NTRIP (Networked Transport of RTCM via Internet Protocol) from CORS (Continuously Operating Reference Stations) or from a self-deployed base station for remote locations.




SyoP

SyoP (Surveying on Phone) is a powerful, all-in-one mobile application designed to handle every aspect of topographic surveying directly from your smartphone or tablet.
It eliminates the need for bulky survey equipment and complex software setups by integrating GNSS data processing, RTK corrections, data collection, map visualization, and post-processing into a single, user-friendly app.
With SyoP, surveyors can connect their mobile device to a GNSS receiver, perform precise field measurements, collect geospatial data, and generate survey reports—all from their phone.

CamGNSS

CamGNSS is an innovative mobile application that combines high-precision GNSS positioning with your smartphone’s camera to capture photos with centimeter-level accurate geotagging.
Designed for surveying, mapping, asset management, and field data collection, CamGNSS ensures that every photo is accurately tagged with precise coordinates, making it an essential tool for professionals in geospatial industries.
The app integrates with RTK-enabled GNSS receivers for real-time corrections, enabling highly accurate positioning.

Frequently Asked Questions

GNSS stands for Global Navigation Satellite System, encompassing satellite constellations like GPS (USA), GLONASS (Russia), Galileo (EU), and BeiDou (China) that provide global positioning, navigation, and timing services.

GNSS receivers calculate their position by measuring the time it takes for signals from multiple satellites to reach them, determining the distance to each satellite, and triangulating their exact location.

GPS is a specific GNSS developed by the United States. GNSS is a broader term that includes multiple satellite systems like GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, and BeiDou.

Factors such as atmospheric conditions, signal multipath (reflections off buildings or terrain), satellite geometry, and receiver quality can influence GNSS accuracy.

Real-Time Kinematic (RTK) GNSS enhances positioning accuracy by using a stationary base station to provide correction data to the moving receiver, achieving centimeter-level precision.
